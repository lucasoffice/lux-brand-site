Overview

A refresh of a working building.

Two floors, ~32,800 SF, last renovated circa 2007. Floor 1 converts to storage/receiving with limited offices. Floor 2 is a full renovation for watchmakers and offices. Demolition confirmed for standard business hours — no overnight work required.

Observation 01

Two floors, two different scopes.

Floor 1 (~16,400 SF) is 70% warehouse/storage with epoxy-painted concrete and 30% walled offices. Floor 2 (~16,400 SF) is a full renovation for watchmaker workshop and offices with specialty VCT flooring.

Observation 02

Standard-hours demo confirmed.

Jacquelyn confirmed demolition can proceed during the day. No overnight work required. Floor 1 runs as a single full-floor phase (no production to protect). Floor 2 is phased in halves to keep watchmakers working.

Observation 03

Watchmaker VCT flooring.

Observation 04

02
Scope

What's in, what's out.

The base number covers construction and finishes across two floors with different finish profiles. A separate vendor will handle furniture, jeweler stations, and specialty workbenches. Architectural drawings are TAG Heuer's scope.

Included in Base
  • Floor 1 — Storage/receiving + offices — 70% warehouse (epoxy-painted concrete, warehouse lighting, no ACT), 30% walled offices (carpet, paint, ACT).
  • Floor 2 — Watchmaker workshop + offices — full renovation at $56/SF. Specialty VCT flooring for watchmaker areas, carpet in offices.
  • Electrical relocation & manipulation — $90K (Fl. 1, simpler scope) and $130K (Fl. 2).
  • Interior demolition — standard hours, ~$8/SF, one week per phase.
  • Wall construction / reconfiguration — ~$185/LF, 250 LF/floor est.
  • Phasing coordination & site supervision — Lux-led, all phases.
Excluded — Separate Budget
  • Furniture & workstation manipulation — separate vendor.
  • Stainless steel workbenches — equipment budget, client-sourced.
  • Jeweler stations & specialty fixtures — handled independently.
  • Architectural & design drawings — TAG Heuer scope.
  • Union labor premium — estimate assumes open shop.
  • Permits (if required) — $15K–$20K allowance noted separately.
03
Approach

Full floor or half. Standard hours. Three phases.

All work — including demolition — runs on standard business hours. Floor 1 runs as a single full-floor phase (warehouse/storage, no active production). Floor 2 is phased in halves to keep watchmakers working on the untouched side.

01

Half-floor phasing

Three phases — Floor 1 full-floor (no production to protect), Floor 2 in halves — with a one-week overlap between phases. Watchmakers stay live on the untouched half.

02

Standard-hours demolition

One week per phase, standard hours, with dust containment and HVAC isolation from active zones.

03

Open shop, standard hours

Framing, finishes, electrical, and punch on normal hours with open-shop labor.

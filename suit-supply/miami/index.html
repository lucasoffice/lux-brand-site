03
Method

Four operating commitments through turnover.

How this job is run day-to-day: accountability, buyout discipline, work-hours logic, and reporting cadence.

01

Single point of accountability.

One project executive and one site superintendent assigned full-time for the duration. They own schedule, budget, and the daily relationship with the Suit Supply construction lead. No rotating staff, no subcontracted supervision.

02

Early buyout inside two weeks.

Long-lead items — brand millwork, light fixtures, and HVAC equipment — locked within the first two weeks of award. Buyout calls follow drawing release. Procurement log issued weekly with vendor, lead time, and on-site required-by date.

03

Work hours coordinated to the block.

Permitted work hours confirmed with the landlord and surrounding Miami tenants on week one. Loud demo, delivery windows, and after-hours work sequenced around neighbor operating hours so complaints stay off the superintendent's desk.

04

Friday reporting package.

Weekly package issued every Friday: dated photo log, three-week look-ahead, RFI log, submittal log, and budget variance against the line items in this document. One email, one PDF, one source of truth for the Suit Supply team.

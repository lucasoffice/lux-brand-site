02
Approach — 02

Four moves to protect the plaster, the schedule, and the mock-up.

Margiela's raw plaster, white-wash, and hand-finished vocabulary live or die on tolerance and sequencing. These four moves front-load the risk.

0—01

Early Buyout of Long-Lead

Plaster applicator, stone, and millwork locked within the first two weeks of award. Long-lead tails released before design hand-off so float lives in the schedule, not in the trades.

0—02

Finish Mock-Ups

On-site plaster and paint mock-ups approved by brand before full application. Rework eliminated at the panel stage, not discovered at walk-through.

0—03

Landlord-Coordinated Hours

Noisy work scheduled to landlord rules. After-hours premium carried only where required, never as a blanket line.

0—04

Single Point of Accountability

One PM and one Super from award through punch. No hand-offs mid-project, no introduction emails between phases.

03
Read of the Work — 03

What a Margiela store in Miami actually demands.

Four observations shaping the price and the sequence — brand language, climate, landlord rules, and long-lead finishes.

0—01   Brand Language

Raw plaster, exposed structure, deliberate imperfection.

Margiela's retail vocabulary relies on raw plaster, exposed structure, hand-painted white finishes, and deliberate imperfection. Execution tolerances and finish mock-ups — not spec sheets — drive quality.

0—02   Miami Context

Humidity, hurricane code, submarket TBD.

Humidity, hurricane code, and landlord work letters in Miami (Design District, Bal Harbour, or Aventura TBD) affect MEP, envelope, and permitting sequencing. Plaster cure windows and HVAC balancing move on the climate, not on the calendar.

0—03   Landlord & Permitting

City of Miami and Miami-Dade timelines pending.

Landlord criteria, after-hours rules, and City of Miami / Miami-Dade permit timelines need confirmation before a hard schedule can be committed. The work letter and the permit clock are the two gates on any committed GMP.

0—04   Specialty Finishes

Plaster, millwork, and stone drive long-lead.

Signature plaster, custom millwork, and imported stone and metal packages drive long-lead risk and require early buyout. Every day saved on the schedule is bought in the first two weeks after award.

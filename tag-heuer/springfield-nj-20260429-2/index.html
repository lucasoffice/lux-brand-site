Overview

49,200 SF across three floors, scope reduced to two.

Multi-floor office and workshop renovation at TAG Heuer Springfield. Workflow-driven, not aesthetic. Off-hours demo, six-phase sequencing, finishes-level scope.

Anchor 01 · Footprint

Four teams, one renovation.

49,200 SF total across three floors at ~16,400 SF each. Active scope reduced to Floors 1 and 2 per Chris, RVP Operations. Affected groups — jewelers, watchmakers, polishing, operations/packaging.

Anchor 02 · Number

The defensible figure between two.

Operations modeled $1.5–2M; the President modeled ~$5M. This document lands $4,186,770 as the conservative ceiling — full-floor assumption, 250 LF walls/floor, $130K electrical/floor.

Anchor 03 · Posture

Rough costing, not formal bid.

Confidentiality holds across the named stakeholder group. Drawings have not been commissioned. The figure carries leadership to a go/no-go decision before A&D fees commit.

Anchor 04 · Voice

Workflow drives every line.

Floor 1 freight elevator forces stock to be hand-carried from Floor 2 storage. Disconnected office pockets break sightlines between watchmakers and operations. The renovation resolves circulation.

02
Scope

Eight items in, five items out.

Finishes-level GC scope with technical spec callouts. Furniture, stainless workbenches, and architectural drawings excluded by design.

Included — GC Scope
  1. 01Demolition. Off-hours interior finish demo, ~$8/SF, ~1 week per phase.
  2. 02Carpet tile. ~20"×20" office product at $9.00/SF installed plus 15% adhesive.
  3. 03Specialty white rubberized VCT. Watchmaker zones, client-supplied spec, drop-visible bounce-rated.
  4. 04Epoxy over prepped concrete. ~70% of Floor 1 storage consolidation area.
  5. 05Partition walls. ~$185/LF, 250 LF estimate per floor pending plan confirmation.
  6. 06Paint and ACT. Full paint at $56/SF labor; ACT patched and salvaged where possible.
  7. 07Electrical relocation. $130K per floor lump sum; new wall and workstation positions.
  8. 08Phasing logistics. Six phases, ~5 dumpsters per phase, freight elevator (Floor 1 only), 1-week dedicated punch.
Excluded — Separate Scope
  1. 01Furniture procurement. Vendor-led specification and placement, separate budget.
  2. 02Stainless workbenches and jeweler stations. Specialty equipment, equipment budget.
  3. 03Architectural and design drawings. Client-side scope; Lux supports spec generation only.
  4. 04Permit-driven scope expansion. ~$15–20K drawing allowance if filing triggered.
  5. 05Sprinkler modifications. Flagged as permit vulnerability if walls shift heads.
03
Approach

Off-hours demo, daytime build, six half-floor phases.

Phasing logic engineered around confidentiality and continuous operations. Budget locked before drawings.

01

Off-Hours Demo, Daytime Build

Demo runs nights and weekends to maintain confidentiality. Framing, finishes, and electrical proceed on standard hours to control labor cost.

02

Six-Phase Half-Floor Sequencing

Two phases per floor with ~1-week overlaps. Trades flow continuously while operational areas stay live on the untouched half.

03

Matterport Off-Hours Capture

Digital twin captured outside business hours. Stakeholders review the space remotely without raising visibility internally.

04

Shared Hub As Single Record

This microsite serves Chris, Brian, and Jacquelyn as a single source of truth. Inputs land asynchronously; no game of telephone.

05

Decoupled Furniture Vendor

Base GC pricing stays clean. Furniture vendor specs and prices specialty workstations under a separate line.

06

Budget First, Drawings Second

Credible ballpark delivered now. Leadership decides go/no-go before architectural fees commit to paper.

Two non-negotiables holding the estimate.

Confidentiality posture and budget defensibility — both fixed before any drawing or trade contract.

01

Confidentiality is non-negotiable.

All site activity, including initial walkthrough and demo phases, runs off-hours. No vendor signage, no daytime crew presence, no internal communications outside the named stakeholder group.

02

Budget defensibility over precision.

Estimate built on conservative assumptions — full-floor scope, 250 LF walls per floor, $130K electrical per floor. Leadership receives a credible ceiling rather than a low number eroded by change orders.

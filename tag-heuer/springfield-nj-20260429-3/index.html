02
Scope

Ten line items in. Five line items out.

Selective demo, drywall partitions per master-watchmaker markup, three flooring systems, paint, ACT patching, and electrical relocation. Furniture, stainless workstations, and architectural drawings carried separately.

Included
  1. Selective demolitionInterior finishes, partitions, ceiling reconfiguration. Floor 1 broader for storage conversion.
  2. Drywall partitionsPer master-watchmaker markup. ~250 LF/floor placeholder pending final layout.
  3. Carpet tile — offices20"×20" tile, ~$9/SF installed, +15% adhesive material.
  4. Rubberized white VCT — watchmakersDrop-visible, bounce-not-break. Spec from client pending.
  5. Epoxy paint over scraped slab~70% of Floor 1 for warehouse and storage zones.
  6. Paint — base coverageAll renovated areas. No wallpaper. Carried in base labor rate.
  7. ACT salvage and patchExisting acoustical grid reused where feasible. Patch budget only.
  8. Electrical relocationLump sum per floor. Manipulation to suit new wall layout.
  9. Phased six-segment executionHalf-floor at a time. One-week overlap between adjacent phases.
  10. Dumpsters and waste handling~5 per phase budgeted to cover swap-outs.
Excluded
  1. Furniture manipulationDeferred to separate vendor once design solidifies.
  2. Stainless steel workstationsWatchmaker, jeweler benches under separate equipment budget.
  3. Architectural and design drawingsClient-engaged. Required if permit triggered.
  4. PermitsApproach classifies as finishes-only. Trigger adds ~$15–20K drawing cost.
  5. Sprinkler modificationsOut of base scope. Disturbed heads revisit scope and permit path.

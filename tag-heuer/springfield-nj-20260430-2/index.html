Overview

Two floors, 32,800 square feet, six phases, 33 weeks.

Multi-floor office and workshop renovation at TAG Heuer's Springfield, NJ facility. Floor 1 — storage, receiving, epoxy across ~70% of plate. Floor 2 — watchmaker workshop, jeweler benches, polishing, packaging, offices.

Scope refined from three floors to two per Chris's operations call. Floor 3 retained in the rollup for reference only and de-scoped going forward.

04
Operational Constraints

Freight elevator serves Floor 1 only. Watchmaker VCT must bounce, not break.

Three structural constraints shape the plan — vertical material handling, dropped-component visibility, and confidentiality with internal staff. Each produced a specific scope or sequencing decision.

Constraint 01 · Vertical Handling

Freight elevator on Floor 1 only.

Floor 2 storage is currently hand-carried down stairs and repacked before shipping. Renovation must address material flow between watchmaker (Floor 2) and packaging/shipping (Floor 1) within the new layout.

Constraint 02 · Component Visibility

White rubber VCT in watchmaker zones.

Spec selected so dropped components are visible against white ground and bounce rather than shatter. Cost comparable to carpet, functionally critical. Spec to be issued by Chris / Operations.

Constraint 03 · Confidentiality

Internal team not yet aware.

Springfield staff have not been briefed on renovation planning. Site walkthrough off-hours. Introductions held until Jacquelyn authorizes. All documentation audience-controlled.

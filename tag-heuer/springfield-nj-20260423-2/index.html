01
Overview

Two floors, 32,800 square feet, six phases reduced to four.

Renovation of the TAG Heuer Springfield facility — floors 1 and 2 of a three-floor, 49,200 SF building. Floor 3 removed from scope per Chris on April 15. Phased execution keeps jewelers, watchmakers, and polishing operational throughout.

The original brief covered three floors, six phases, and a budget range that diverged widely between stakeholders inside TAG Heuer. The president estimated $5M. The operations team estimated $1.0 to $2.0M. Jacquelyn Albano engaged Lux Brand Co to produce a defensible ballpark before any architectural drawings were commissioned, working from the master watchmaker's schematic sketches and blue-line wall markups. No formal plans existed. Three different second-floor options remained in play pending team alignment between operations, the master watchmaker, and executive review.

On the April 15 call, Chris reduced the scope materially. Floor 3 was removed entirely from the renovation. Floor 1 was reclassified to approximately 70% warehouse with epoxy paint on scraped slab and approximately 30% office with carpet tile and ACT. Daytime demolition was confirmed acceptable on the reduced footprint, eliminating the off-hours labor premium from the original assumption. Floor 2 remained the full watchmaker and jeweler workshop renovation with specialty white rubberized VCT in the watchmaker zones — specification pending from the TAG Heuer watchmaker team. The freight elevator serves only Floor 1, which forces manual pallet movement between levels and makes workflow reconfiguration the core problem rather than aesthetics.

The deliverable is a defensible ballpark before architectural drawings are commissioned. All published figures currently reflect the three-floor rollup with 12% overhead and 5% contingency applied. A revised two-floor rollup issues after the off-hours site walkthrough, confirmed linear footage of walls, and the finalized VCT flooring specification. The finishes-only posture holds unless sprinkler modifications or electrical circuit thresholds trigger a full permit filing, which would add approximately $15,000 to $20,000 in drawings plus filing fees to the base number.

Lux Brand Co has prior TAG Heuer execution history from the Miami engagement with Claudia as owner's rep, plus corporate precedent on Omega watchmaker stations and phased Super Value office renovations. Sean leads construction on the Lux Brand Co side with existing subcontractor relationships across North Central New Jersey and prior Short Hills market experience. The team is built for phased finishes work on active workspaces where continuity of operations is the primary constraint on the schedule.

Permit Posture

Finishes-only posture holds until sprinkler or electrical trips it.

Two vulnerabilities gate the permit decision — sprinkler modifications and electrical circuit thresholds. If either triggers, add approximately $15,000 to $20,000 in drawings plus permit fees to the base number.

Block 01 — Current Posture

Finishes-only. No drawings required. No permit filed.

Work positioned as finishes-only scope — demolition, partition walls, flooring, paint, ACT patch, and electrical relocation without panel modification. Finishes-only posture assumes jurisdictional review does not require full construction documents or a filed permit application for the work described.

Status — Held
Block 02 — Vulnerability 01

Sprinkler modifications.

If partition reconfiguration shifts sprinkler head locations or requires new heads in reconfigured zones, the work triggers a full permit filing. Lux electrician and fire protection subcontractor review the existing head layout against the proposed partition markup before filing posture is locked.

Status — Pending Lux Electrician
Block 03 — Vulnerability 02

Electrical thresholds.

If circuit count or panel modification exceeds the jurisdictional threshold for finishes-only work, the scope triggers full permit filing. The $130,000 per floor electrical relocation is a bare-bones assumption — Lux electrician confirms whether proposed work stays below the threshold or crosses it.

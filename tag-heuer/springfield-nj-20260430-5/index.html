Overview
TAG HEUERSWISS AVANT-GARDE SINCE 1860

Two floors, 32,800 SF, six phases, standard hours.

Multi-floor renovation of TAG Heuer's Springfield, NJ watchmaking and operations facility. Scope revised from three floors to two — Floor 1 storage and receiving, Floor 2 watchmaker workshop and offices.

01

Why the scope exists.

Flow breaks between jewelers, watchmakers, polishing, and packaging. Freight elevator reaches Floor 1 only. Crates move floor-to-floor by hand.

02

What changed in April.

Chris, RVP Operations, reduced scope from three floors to two. Floor 1 becomes storage and receiving. Floor 2 is the watchmaker renovation.

03

What this document is.

A defensible budget range. Aligns the $1.5M operations estimate against the $5M president-level estimate ahead of architect engagement.

Confidentiality Note Project remains internal to TAG Heuer leadership. Site walkthrough scheduled off-hours. Matterport scan under consideration for stakeholder review.
02
Scope

Demolition, walls, flooring, electrical relocation — finishes-only posture.

Seven scope items confirmed inside the GC line. Four items carved out for separate vendor or architect tracks.

Included — GC Line
  1. Interior demolition — standard hours, ~$8/SFOffice finishes demo per revised plan; no overnight premium.
  2. Wall construction — ~250 LF/floor at ~$185/LFNew partitions and selective removals to open dead space.
  3. White VCT-rubber flooring in watchmaker zonesDrop-visibility spec; bounce-not-break; client-supplied.
  4. Floor 1 epoxy-painted concrete — ~70% of floorWarehouse and receiving area finish.
  5. ACT — patch and salvage where possibleSelective replacement around new wall locations.
  6. Electrical relocation — lump sum per floorExisting-circuit manipulation to support new layout.
  7. Six-phase logistics — ~5 dumpsters per phaseHalf-floor sequencing with ~1 week overlap.
Excluded — Separate Track
  1. Furniture and workstation manipulationSeparate vendor procurement runs parallel.
  2. Stainless steel jeweler benchesSpecialty equipment outside GC scope; client-sourced.
  3. Architectural drawings — $15–20K if permits requiredTAG Heuer engages architect separately.
  4. Permit feesSprinkler or major electrical changes trigger filing.

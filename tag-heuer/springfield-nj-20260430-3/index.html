02
Scope

Finishes-driven renovation, permit-avoidance posture.

Floor 1 absorbs storage at 70% epoxy concrete and 30% carpeted offices. Floor 2 reconfigures for watchmakers on white VCT rubber.

Included
  1. Demolition — standard hours, ~$8/SF × 16,400 SF per floor
  2. Wall construction — ~$185/LF, 250 LF/floor pending plan confirmation
  3. Electrical relocation — lump sum ~$130,000 per floor, no service upgrades
  4. Floor 1 epoxy — ~70% epoxy paint over scraped/prepped concrete
  5. Floor 1 carpet — ~30% carpet tile in built-out office zones
  6. Floor 2 VCT — specialty white rubber flooring, watchmaker areas, spec from client
  7. Carpet tile — 20"×20" tiles at ~$9/SF installed, office zones
  8. Adhesive — 15% material uplift on carpet, both floors
  9. Paint & ACT — included in $56/SF finishes labor, grid salvaged where possible
  10. Dumpsters & punch — five dumpsters per phase, one-week punch closeout per phase
Excluded
  1. Furniture manipulation — separate vendor, post-design
  2. Stainless workbenches & jeweler stations — equipment budget
  3. Architectural & design drawings — TAG Heuer client scope
  4. Permit + full architectural set — finishes scope avoids; ~$15K–$20K if triggered
  5. Floor 3 — removed from scope per Chris
  6. Wallpaper — paint only, client confirmed
  7. MEP service upgrades — relocation only, no new capacity

01
Project Brief

A 32,800 SF two-floor reflow for the Springfield watchmaker and jeweler teams.

Originally scoped as three floors, now locked to Floors 1 and 2. Budget delivered ahead of drawings so TAG Heuer leadership can close a $1.5M-to-$5M internal spread.

The facility sits in Springfield, New Jersey. Scope was reduced from three floors to two on the follow-up call — Floor 1 consolidates in/out pallet storage and a small office cluster, Floor 2 houses the watchmaker benches, the polishing room, the jeweler stations, and a new office footprint. Total area in scope is 32,800 SF. The purpose of this document is to ground an internal budget gap between the Ops number at $1.5M and the President's number at $5M before leadership commits capital.

Jacquelyn Albano leads the project on the TAG Heuer side, coordinating input from the VP of Operations, RVP Chris, and the master watchmaker. The April 2026 planning call established the reduced footprint, the day-shift labor allowance, and the open-shop trade base. This is a rough costing exercise. No architectural drawings exist yet. Three schematic options for Floor 2 are still being weighed internally. The numbers here are defensible within the variables called out in Section 09.

Included and excluded scope stated as a matched pair so the furniture vendor and architect lanes are visible from the first read.

03
Delivery Approach

Six phases, half a floor at a time, daytime labor confirmed, open-shop trades out of North Jersey.

Phasing runs in half-a-floor increments with ~1 week overlap between phases so the jeweler and watchmaker teams stay operational through the build. Day-shift labor is confirmed for all trades including demo, which removes the off-hours premium carried in the original estimate. Open-shop non-union trades are confirmed with client and Lux has a stable North Jersey base in that lane. The budget precedes drawings deliberately so leadership can make a go/no-go call before the three competing Floor 2 schematics collapse to one.

04
Phasing Logic

Floor 1 first, Floor 2 second, two-week holiday buffer absorbs slip.

The freight elevator only reaches Floor 1 — sequencing Floor 1 warehouse consolidation first unlocks clean staging for Floor 2 watchmaker work.

Floor 1 runs before Floor 2 because the freight elevator terminates on Floor 1. The current workflow forces staff to unpack, carry down stairs, recrate, and re-freight — a flow the renovation is designed to eliminate. Opening Floor 1 storage flow first also stages materials and debris handling for the Floor 2 watchmaker, polishing, and jeweler reflow that follows.

The structure is five working phases plus a buffer — Floor 1 First Half, Floor 1 Second Half, Floor 2 First Half, Floor 2 Second Half, and a two-week holiday float at the end. Each working phase is six weeks. Total duration is 33 weeks from the Floor 1 demo start to punch close on the buffer.

Phase overlap is ~1 week, not zero. Adjacent phases share finishes and punch time.

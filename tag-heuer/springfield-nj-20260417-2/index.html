01   Project Brief

01A 49,200 SF watchmaker and operations renovation across Floors 1 and 2.

Springfield, NJ. Scoped April 2026 for Jacquelyn Albano and Chris, RVP Operations. Prepared to close a $1M–$5M internal estimating gap with a single defensible number.

The project covers interior demolition, wall reconfiguration, flooring, paint, ACT patching, and electrical relocation across Floors 1 and 2. Floor 3 was removed from scope on 4/15 per Chris, RVP Operations; first-floor demo absorbs storage currently living on Floor 2. Watchmaker zones receive specialty white rubberized VCT; offices receive carpet tile; warehouse zones receive grind-and-paint epoxy. This document exists to resolve the spread between the president's $5M estimate and operations' $1M–$2M with one credible budgetary number leadership can decide against.

02   Scope of Work

02Nine trades in, four trades out.

Base-build finishes only. Specialty workstations, formal drawings, permit fees, and Floor 3 are carved out and routed elsewhere.

Included — 9 items
  • Interior demolition.Full demo of Floor 1 to open storage. Selective demo on Floor 2 for watchmaker reconfiguration. Performed during day hours per client confirmation on 4/15.
  • Wall reconfiguration.New partition walls per master watchmaker markup. Floor 1 wall count is roughly half of Floor 2. Assumed 250 LF per floor pending plan confirmation.
  • Flooring — VCT.Specialty white rubberized VCT in watchmaker zones on Floor 2. Allows dropped parts to be seen and bounces them to prevent breakage. Per client-provided spec.
  • Flooring — carpet tile.20x20 carpet tile at roughly $9/SF installed equivalent. Office areas on both floors. Material and adhesive line-itemed separately in pricing.
  • Flooring — epoxy paint.Grind-and-paint epoxy finish across roughly 70% of Floor 1 for warehouse / storage use. Remaining 30% receives office finishes.
  • Paint.Coat of paint throughout renovated areas on both floors. No wallpaper required. Colors per client selection during design development.
  • ACT ceiling patch.Patch and repair existing ACT grid where walls are relocated. Salvage and reuse tile where possible to keep existing condition consistent.
  • Electrical relocation.Manipulation of existing electrical to support new wall layouts and workstation positions. Budgeted as lump sum per floor at $130,000.
  • Phased execution.Work sequenced in four half-floor phases under the revised 2-floor scope. Roughly 1-week overlap between phases preserves operational continuity.
Excluded — 4 items
  • Furniture and workstations.Specialty stainless steel workstations, jeweler benches, and furniture procurement / manipulation handled by a separate vendor on a separate proposal.
  • Architectural drawings.Formal design drawings are client-provided. Lux will assist in developing build-spec input but is not scoping architectural services in this number.
  • Permit allowance.Work positioned as finishes-only to avoid permit. If sprinkler or electrical thresholds trigger a permit, drawings and fees (~$15K–$20K) are added via change order.
  • Third floor.Removed from scope per Chris on 4/15. Floor 3 pricing is retained in the estimate as reference from the initial three-floor exercise, not as an active line.
03   Method

03Ballpark first. Daytime work. Four half-floor phases. Furniture decoupled.

Seven decisions that shape the number on the last page.

01

Ballpark first, refine against plans.

Deliver a defensible budgetary range now so leadership can decide whether to proceed. Refinement follows once linear wall footage, VCT spec, and electrical scope are confirmed against real drawings.

02

Daytime work confirmed.

Client confirmed demo and construction can run during standard hours. Removes the off-hours labor premium carried in the initial exercise and materially improves the number.

03

Phased by half-floor.

Originally six phases. Under the revised 2-floor scope, phasing condenses to four half-floor phases of six weeks each with roughly one-week overlap between phases.

04

Budget the full 16,400 SF per floor.

Rather than carving out the two-thirds Chris mentioned, the full floor is budgeted. Restrooms and untouched zones absorb into margin and protect the number against surprise overruns.

05

Furniture decoupled from GC scope.

GC proposal stays clean — base build only. A separate furniture vendor proposes workstations and benches independently. Owner can flex design without reopening construction pricing.

06

Off-hours walkthrough / Matterport.

Full site captured digitally so all stakeholders review remotely. Protects the confidentiality of the planning phase and avoids alerting on-site staff during operating hours.

07

Micro-site for async input.

Stakeholder comments collected asynchronously through the shared project micro-site. Reduces the game-of-telephone revision loop and protects Jacquelyn's calendar from coordination overhead.

04   Phasing Logic

04Half a floor at a time, one-week overlaps, operations never fully dark.

Springfield stays in production throughout. Phase boundaries were drawn against the jeweler / watchmaker / polishing workflow, not against calendar convenience.

Half-floor phasing holds because full-floor phasing breaks operations. Jewelers, watchmakers, and polishing teams need continuous access to tools and unfinished work. A half-floor approach keeps half the workforce in production while the other half is relocated around the active construction zone.

The freight elevator serves Floor 1 only, which constrains material staging and makes full-floor shutdowns on Floor 2 operationally unworkable. A one-week overlap between phases absorbs finishes cure time and punch, and gives operations a buffer to migrate staff back into the completed half before the next half opens up.

09   Guardrails

Furniture and permit carved out.

Specialty stainless workstations and jeweler benches are excluded and routed to a separate vendor. Permit and drawing fees of roughly $15K–$20K are not in the base and only trigger if sprinkler or electrical scope crosses permit thresholds.

01 / Scope

Two floors at ~16,400 SF each.

Floor 1 demo-heavy, epoxy over scraped concrete on ~70% of the slab. Floor 2 carries watchmaker, jeweler, polishing, and offices on specialty white VCT and carpet tile.

Workflow, not aesthetics.

Freight elevator reaches Floor 1 only, forcing manual repacking from Floor 2 storage. Renovation consolidates storage on Floor 1 and rebuilds Floor 2 around watchmaker, jeweler, polishing, and shipping flow.

Approach

Six phases, half-floor at a time, standard hours confirmed.

Daytime work permitted per Jacquelyn, 4/15 call. ~1 week overlap between phases. Permit-avoidance posture monitored against sprinkler and electrical thresholds.

01

Phasing locked at half-floor.

Six phases, each running half a floor. Watchmakers, jewelers, and polishing remain at the bench on the untouched half. ~1 week overlap absorbs handoff between phases without halting production.

02

Daytime work confirmed.

Per Jacquelyn on the 4/15 call, no overnight shifts required. Demo and trades run on standard hours. Phase 1 demo runs off-hours by exception only.

03

ACT salvage approved.

Existing acoustical ceiling tile and grid date to the 2007 renovation but remain serviceable. Reuse where walls move; patch and replace selectively. Holds ACT to a patch line item.

04

Two-track flooring locked.

White rubberized VCT only in watchmaker zones — dropped components stay visible and bounce. Carpet tile in offices. Epoxy over scraped concrete on ~70% of Floor 1.

05

Permit-avoidance posture.

Scope positioned as finishes work. Two vulnerabilities monitored: sprinkler modifications if walls intersect the existing grid, and electrical scope if circuit count crosses threshold. Either trigger converts the $15K–$20K drawings line to add.

This number lands before architectural drawings. Leadership decides go / no-go on a defensible figure rather than waiting on a full set. Drawings procured downstream.

03
Approach

Half-floor phasing, day-shift labor, open shop, permit avoidance.

Six half-floor phases with one-week overlap. Standard hours confirmed by Jacquelyn. Non-union jurisdiction confirmed; NJ subs mobilized from prior Short Hills and Springfield work.

3.1 — Phasing math

Six half-floor pulls, six weeks each.

1 wk demo / 2 wks build-sand / 2 wks finishes / 1 wk punch. One-week overlap between consecutive phases compresses 36 gross weeks to 26 calendar.

3.2 — Day-shift labor

Standard hours, no overnight premium.

Jacquelyn confirmed business-hours work, including demo. Off-hours premium retained as contingency only, not baseline.

3.3 — Open shop

Non-union jurisdiction, NJ sub base.

Subcontractors mobilized from prior Short Hills and Springfield projects. No union premium baked into the rollup.

3.4 — Permit avoidance

Filed as finishes work.

$15–20K drawings contingency held if sprinkler or electrical thresholds trigger a permit. Lux electrician validates before filing.

3.5 — Discreet capture

Off-hours walk or Matterport.

Initial site capture done quietly so Springfield staff are not alerted. Matterport enables remote stakeholder review.

3.6 — Live calibration

Micro-site replaces relay.

Jacquelyn, Chris, and Brian post requirements asynchronously. Lux compiles and republishes within 48 hours.

3.7 — Furniture decoupled

GC scope clean of FF&E.

Furniture vendor scoped separately. Construction line item stays clean and change-order risk is contained.

Discretion required · site walkthroughs coordinated off-hours or low-visibility per client request

  • Interior demolition.Selective demo of existing walls, finishes, and ACT across ~16,400 SF per floor. ~1 week per floor, off-hours, crew-sized to phase.
  • New wall construction.Reconfigured partitions per schematic markups. Budget carries ~250 LF per floor at ~$185/LF pending plan confirmation.

03
Execution Logic

Budget first, drawings later, operations never off-line.

Seven decisions that shape how the number was built and how the work will land on site.

01

Budget-first, drawings-later.

The President and Operations are ~$3M apart. A credible ballpark off schematic markups lets leadership decide whether to proceed before a full design package gets commissioned. Drawings follow an approved number, not the other way round.

02

Six-phase half-floor sequence.

Jewelers, watchmakers, polishing, and packaging cannot be displaced at the same time. Half-floor phasing with ~1-week overlaps keeps every team running while the adjacent half is being rebuilt.

03

Off-hours Matterport tied to confidentiality.

Broader staff are not yet aware of the renovation. The scan runs outside operating hours and becomes the shared visual reference Chris, Brian, and Jacquelyn work off — no on-site walkthrough required to align.

04

Micro-site replaces the telephone game.

Chris, Brian, and Jacquelyn have been contributing scope input across separate calls. This document — and its input form — is the single surface where context lands so the same number reaches every stakeholder without relay drift.

05

Furniture runs on a separate vendor track.

Workstation manipulation and stainless jeweler stations are out of GC scope. A furniture vendor proposes layouts and specialty equipment in parallel, keeping the construction box clean and priceable.

06

Open-shop North Jersey trade base.

Springfield is non-union jurisdiction. Subcontractor relationships were already built out on the prior Short Hills corporate work and SuperValu HQ projects — the same bench prices this job, which materially lowers the labor rate.

07

Finishes-work positioning for permits.

Documentation and scope are structured to stay below the full-permit threshold. Sprinkler head relocation and electrical service upsizing are the two vulnerabilities — both get verified with the MEP sub before the position is locked in.

04
Occupied-Building Protocol

Half a floor at a time, one-week overlap, demo after hours.

The facility remains operational throughout. Demo runs off-hours; build, finishes, and punch run on standard hours to protect the labor rate.

Why half-floor phasing. Jewelers, watchmakers, polishing, and packaging cannot be displaced simultaneously — each team's work-in-progress is physical, insured, and logistically linked to the freight elevator that serves Floor 1 only. A half-floor window lets one team shift laterally to the completed half while the adjacent half is under construction, without off-site relocation.

Why demo off-hours, balance on standard hours. Demolition drives the dust, noise, and debris load — isolating it to after-hours removes the operational conflict without carrying overtime on the full job. Build-out, finishes, and punch all run on standard hours where the rate is materially lower. Overtime is applied only where it pays back, not as a blanket.

The overlap mechanic. Each phase carries roughly a one-week overlap with the next. The following crew mobilizes and begins demo while punch closes on the prior half, which collapses the gross schedule without compressing any individual phase. Sequence inside each half is fixed: Demo (1 wk) → Build/Sand (2 wks) → Finishes (2 wks) → Punch (1 wk).

Reading This Document

Two caveats before the number is quoted upstream.

Guardrail 01

Preliminary, not a bid.

Figures are built on schematic markups and verbal scope. The ~$4.19M base excludes furniture, equipment, drawings, and permit costs, and assumes 250 LF of wall per floor — which is the single largest remaining variable and the line item most exposed to revision once plans are issued.

Guardrail 02

Scope has already shifted once.

Between the first and second calls, scope dropped from three floors to two, with Floor 1 largely converted to warehouse and ~30% retained as offices. Numbers in this document still reflect the 3-floor assumption and will be reissued against the revised scope once confirmed.

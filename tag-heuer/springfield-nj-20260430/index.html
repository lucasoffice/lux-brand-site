01
Overview

Two floors, 32,800 SF, six phases, watchmakers stay working.

TAG Heuer's Springfield, NJ facility consolidates storage to Floor 1 and rebuilds the watchmaker workshop and offices on Floor 2. Floor 3 deferred per Chris, RVP Operations. No overnight work required.

The renovation closes a $1.5M–$5M internal budget gap with a credible middle number. Floor 1 becomes storage and receiving. Floor 2 becomes workshop and offices.

01 · Workflow

Freight reaches Floor 1 only.

Storage lives on Floor 2. Crates get unpacked, carried down, repacked, then loaded to freight.

02 · Resolved scope

Storage down. Workshop up.

Floor 1 consolidates storage plus a few offices. Floor 2 holds workshop, jeweler, polishing, and offices.

03 · Budget gap

$5M from President. $1.5–2M from Ops.

This document lands the credible middle: $4.19M across two floors, defensible against the spread.

Building Condition

Last renovated circa 2007. Assume traditional ACT grid throughout. Salvage and patch where possible; coat of paint everywhere.

02
Scope

Demolition, walls, flooring, paint, ACT, electrical relocation.

Nine line items in. Five line items out. Specialty white rubberized VCT non-negotiable in watchmaker zones — drops must be visible and must bounce.

Included
  1. Demolition — interior office finishes.$8/SF, ~1 week per phase, off-hours.
  2. Wall construction — partition reconfiguration.$185/LF per master watchmaker blue-line markups.
  3. Watchmaker VCT — white, rubberized.Spec from client. Drops visible. Drops bounce.
  4. Carpet tile — 20"×20" in office areas.$25/tile, $9/SF installed. +15% adhesive.
  5. Epoxy paint on concrete — Floor 1.~70% of Floor 1, warehouse/storage zones.
  6. Paint and ACT patching — building-wide.Included in $56/SF labor rate.
  7. Electrical relocation — outlets, switches, circuits.$130,000 per floor allowance.
  8. Six-phase execution — half-floor sequencing.~1-week overlap between phases.
  9. Site logistics — dumpsters and trash.~5 dumpsters per phase budgeted.
Excluded
  1. Furniture and workstation manipulation.Separate vendor handles placement.
  2. Stainless steel workbenches and jeweler stations.Separate equipment budget, client-sourced.
  3. Architectural drawings.Client scope. ~$15–20K if permit set required.
  4. Permit fees — full code submission.Sprinkler and electrical thresholds to confirm.
  5. Wallpaper and specialty wall finishes.Paint only. Confirmed by Jacquelyn.

Approach

Six phases, half-floor at a time, demo off-hours.

Operations migrate progressively across the building. No hard shutdown. Internal staff not informed until leadership signs off.

Workflow Logic

Freight serves Floor 1. Storage moves to Floor 1. Workshop moves up.

The renovation is a flow problem before it is a finish problem. Crates currently get unpacked, carried down stairs, repacked, and loaded to freight.

Before · Today
  • Freight elevator reaches Floor 1 only.
  • Storage lives on Floor 2 — wrong floor.
  • Watchmakers, jewelers, polishing, ops fragmented across floors.
  • Dead space between teams.
After · Resolved
  • Floor 1: consolidated storage, receiving, a few offices.
  • Floor 2: watchmaker workshop, jeweler, polishing, offices.
  • Freight path direct from receiving to storage.
  • Adjacent teams adjacent.

Guardrails

Confidential until signed off. Furniture carved out.

Two non-negotiables protect the scope and the client.

01 · Confidential

No on-site exposure.

All site visits, scans, and pre-construction activity scheduled off-hours. Internal Springfield staff are not made aware of the project until TAG Heuer leadership signs off.

02 · Carve-out

Furniture and equipment out of GC scope.

Stainless workbenches, jeweler stations, and furniture handled by a separate vendor. Protects the GC bid from specialty equipment risk and keeps the budget comparison apples-to-apples.

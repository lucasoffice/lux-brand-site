Why this exists

Leadership estimates ranged from $1M (Ops) to $5M (President). Jacquelyn Albano needs a defensible number before commissioning architectural drawings.

This document is that number. Built from schematic sketches, 2007 as-built plans with blue-line markups, and a phone-call walkthrough with the master watchmaker.

Revisions follow plan issuance. The site itself is living — Section 08 collects stakeholder notes between meetings.

What's in

Interior demolition on standard hours. Wall framing at 250 LF/floor placeholder. Flooring across three finish profiles — 20"×20" carpet tile, specialty white rubberized VCT, epoxy over scraped slab.

Paint throughout, ACT salvage-and-reset, and electrical relocation at $130K/floor lump. One-week punch window per phase. Five dumpsters per phase including swap-outs.

Open-shop labor base. No union premium carried.

What's out

Specialty jeweler benches and stainless watchmaker stations run under a separate furniture vendor. Architectural drawings are client-side. Permits and expediting carved out at ~$15–20K if triggered.

Third floor descoped per RVP Ops. Floor 3 line items retained in the rollup for leadership reference only — not included in the active scope total.

Design fees and furniture procurement sit outside the GC base.

Scope

Finishes-only across Floors 1 and 2.

Ten line items confirmed in base scope; five carved out to protect the finishes-only permit strategy and keep GC pricing clean of equipment and design fees.

  • Interior demolition — removal of existing walls, finishes, and flooring across Floors 1 and 2. Cleared for standard hours per Chris. ~$8/SF basis.
  • Wall construction — framing, drywall, tape, finish for reconfigured offices and workshop zones. 250 LF/floor placeholder at $185/LF. Final LF pending plans.

  • Electrical relocation — power, data, lighting to match new plan. $130K/floor lump sum placeholder.
  • Dumpsters + debris removal — ~5 dumpsters per phase including swap-outs. Carried in GC base.
  • Punch + closeout — one-week punch window per phase. Final walk per phase, not project-end only.

  • Stainless watchmaker stations — TAG Heuer equipment budget. Client-sourced. Not in GC rollup.
  • Architectural + design drawings — client-side. LBCO assists architect with build spec; no design fees carried. Blocker on item 7 — three competing schematics in play.
  • Permits + expediting — if triggered. ~$15–20K allowance for drawings + filing if sprinkler or electrical load crosses threshold.

Approach

Six half-floor phases, standard hours, no overnight work.

Per RVP Ops, construction runs during standard hours with ~1 week of overlap between phases. Walkthroughs only are off-hours, for confidentiality.

01

Phased execution — six half-floor runs.

Work runs half a floor at a time across six phases. ~1 week of overlap between adjacent phases. Operations remain live throughout — watchmakers stay at their benches on the untouched half while the paired half is under construction. Phases 5 and 6 are held as reference runs on the descoped third floor and are not executed under the active scope.

02

Standard-hours labor, off-hours walkthroughs.

Demolition cleared for daytime per Chris. All trade work — demo, framing, finishes, electrical, punch — runs on standard business hours. No overnight premium carried. The only off-hours activity is the initial site walkthrough, held outside production hours to preserve confidentiality with Jacquelyn coordinating site access.

03

Matterport scan of Floors 1 and 2.

LBCO captures the full space during the walkthrough window. Remote stakeholders — Chris, Brian, the master watchmaker, executive leadership — point to specific zones and describe intent without repeat site visits. The scan becomes the shared reference point between meetings and cuts the number of on-site trips required to finalize scope.

04

Finishes-only permit strategy.

Scope positioned as finishes and FF&E to stay below permit thresholds. Electrician engaged to confirm sprinkler modifications and electrical load do not trigger a filing. If either threshold is crossed, $15–20K is added for drawings and filing fees. Base number assumes no permit.

05

Living budget document.

This micro-site is the working artifact, not a static PDF. Section 08 collects stakeholder notes between meetings — Chris, Brian, the master watchmaker, and executive leadership drop context against specific line items. Luke compiles and revises rather than routing every question through Jacquelyn one at a time.

06

Open-shop labor base.

Client confirmed the Springfield site is non-union. Trade base priced at open-shop rates, which holds labor competitive against the two-floor footprint. No union premium carried. Sean leads subcontractor mobilization out of the Short Hills / Springfield NJ trade network.

04
Constraints

Workflow, freight, and confidentiality drive the plan.

Three project-specific constraints shape phasing and sequencing more than cost does. Each is confirmed on the walkthrough call with Chris and the master watchmaker.

Constraint 01 — Workflow

Poor flow between jewelers, watchmakers, polishing, and ops packaging.

The master watchmaker flagged dead space on Floor 2 and a traffic pattern that forces crossover between polishing and ops packaging. Three competing second-floor schematics exist.

Team alignment on a single schematic is required before architectural drawings are commissioned. That alignment is the pacing item on this engagement.

Constraint 02 — Freight

Freight elevator serves Floor 1 only.

Crated goods currently move by hand from Floor 2 storage to the Floor 1 freight cab. That round-trip consumes ops time every shipping day and creates physical risk for crated inventory.

Consolidating storage to Floor 1 under the new plan resolves the bottleneck without requiring vertical lift infrastructure or a mezzanine addition.

Constraint 03 — Confidentiality

Awareness held tight until scope is solidified.

Jacquelyn has requested off-hours walkthroughs and restricted distribution of this document. Springfield staff are not broadly aware a renovation is being priced.

The Matterport scan substitutes for repeat site visits. No photography of watchmaker stations or active production zones during walkthrough.

Guardrails

No drawings means no fixed price; permit and furniture are carved out.

Two guardrails bound the $4.19M number. Both will be revisited once architectural drawings are issued and the electrician confirms permit thresholds.

01

No drawings = no fixed price.

This exercise is built from schematic sketches, 2007 as-built plans with blue-line markups, and a phone-call walkthrough with the master watchmaker. Architectural drawings do not yet exist.

Three variables carry the largest revision swing once plans are issued:

  • Wall linear footage — 250 LF/floor placeholder at $185/LF.
  • Electrical relocation — $130K/floor lump sum.
  • Specialty white rubberized VCT quantity — allowance within Floor 2 material line.

Revision follows plan issuance. The number moves up or down from $4,186,770 based on these three inputs.

02

Permit and furniture are carved out.

Base number assumes finishes-only work with no permit filing. If sprinkler modifications or electrical load cross threshold, $15–20K is added for drawings and filing fees. Electrician confirmation is item 9 on Next Steps.

Furniture, stainless watchmaker stations, and jeweler workstations run under a separate vendor contract. Design fees and architectural drawings are client-side.

The active scope rollup is labor, demo, walls, flooring, paint, ACT, and electrical relocation — nothing else.

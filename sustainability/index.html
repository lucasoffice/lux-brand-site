SUSTAINABILITY STATEMENT
PUBLISHED JULY 2025
A statement of approach

Sustainability,
built into the work.

LBCo provides this statement to articulate our approach to sustainability as a general contractor specializing in luxury retail build-outs. It describes our values, the practices we apply on projects, and the certifications and standards we are equipped to support when working with brand partners.

01
Our approach

Construction is where sustainability is decided.

LBCo believes that thoughtful construction and supply chain practices are core to delivering quality work in luxury retail. Our role as a general contractor gives us influence over how a space is built — from material selection and site logistics to waste handling and equipment specification — and we use that influence to support the sustainability goals of our clients.

Sustainability at LBCo is collaborative. Brand partners set the standards they want to achieve on a given project — sometimes through formal certification programs like LEED, sometimes through their own internal sustainability frameworks, sometimes through specific scope requirements. We work to understand those goals at the start of every engagement and incorporate them into project planning, procurement, and documentation.

02
How we work with clients

The brand sets the bar; we execute to it.

On projects where a client has formal sustainability requirements, our team works with the client’s design and sustainability teams to ensure those requirements are reflected in scope, vendor packages, and construction documentation.

On projects without formal requirements, we apply the practices below as a baseline where scope and budget allow.
03
Materials & sourcing

What goes into the space matters as much as how it’s built.

We work with brand partners to specify materials that align with their environmental and health objectives. Where local sourcing is feasible, we encourage it as a way to reduce shipping-related emissions.

A
Responsibly sourced wood, including FSC-certified options where the brand specifies it.
B
Paints, coatings, adhesives, and sealants meeting low-VOC standards.
C
Products with recognized environmental and health declarations — EPDs, HPDs, Cradle-to-Cradle, Declare, GreenGuard, FloorScore.
04
Construction waste & site practices

Maximize what gets reused. Minimize what gets thrown away.

For projects where LBCo manages the waste hauler relationship, we develop a project-specific Construction and Demolition Waste Management plan. Our goal is to maximize diversion from landfill and incineration through reuse, donation, recycling, and other higher-value recovery methods, in line with industry standards and our clients’ sustainability targets. Documentation is maintained throughout the project to support reporting and certification submittals.

05
Indoor air quality during construction

The space should be healthy before the doors open.

On projects requiring formal indoor air quality management, we implement a project-specific Construction Indoor Air Quality plan covering containment of dust, VOCs, and other on-site contaminants throughout demolition and construction, and we coordinate appropriate filtration on HVAC systems within scope. Daily logs and supporting photos are maintained for client reporting.

06
Energy & water

Specify well; the savings compound.

We support energy- and water-efficient design choices through specification and procurement. Where within our scope, we specify LED lighting and appropriate controls (dimmers, timers, occupancy and daylight sensors), high-efficiency HVAC and compliant refrigerants, ENERGY STAR-rated IT and appliances, and water-efficient plumbing fixtures.

07
LEED & certification capability

Equipped to deliver under formal certification frameworks.

LBCo has experience delivering boutique projects under the LEED v4 ID+C: Retail certification framework, including coordinating commissioning, materials documentation, waste tracking, and indoor air quality management with the project’s LEED consultant. We are equipped to support LEED Silver, Gold, and Platinum target levels depending on project scope and client objectives.

08
Documentation & reporting

If it’s not documented, it didn’t happen.

LBCo provides clients with project-level documentation suitable for internal sustainability reporting and third-party certification submittals. Standard documentation can include waste management plans and diversion data, indoor air quality logs, FSC certification records, low-VOC product specifications, equipment inventories, and lighting and mechanical drawings indicating compliance details.

09
Continuous improvement

This is a developing area of our practice.

We track changes in industry standards, certification programs, and product availability, and we welcome feedback from brand partners about how we can better support their goals on future projects.

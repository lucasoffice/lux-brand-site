Scope

Six trades included. Four excluded, vendor-direct or landlord-scope.

Scope defined against Chanel boutique standards. Substitutions require written client approval.

Included — LBCO Scope
  1. 2.1General Conditions. Supervision provided. Full-time superintendent on site. Project management, safety program, temporary protection of adjacent tenancies, and end-of-shift cleanup carried through turnover.
  2. 2.2Demolition. Selective demolition confirmed. Existing partitions, finishes, and in-scope MEP removed to slab and deck where required. Disposal and landlord-coordinated haul-off included.
  3. 2.3Millwork and Fixtures. Brand-standard millwork received, protected, and installed. Display fixtures set to Chanel specifications and approved shop drawings. Vendor coordination carried.
  4. 2.4Finishes. Stone and plaster finishes installed. Specialty metal, paint, and floor assemblies delivered to brand standard. On-site mockups built ahead of production runs.
  5. 2.5MEP. Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing modifications executed to support the revised layout, lighting load, and fitting-room count. Tie-in to landlord systems coordinated.
  6. 2.6Lighting. Brand-specified fixtures installed. Control system commissioned. Scene programming performed against Chanel lighting-design approval before turnover.
Excluded — By Others
  1. 2.7FF&E procurement. Furniture, display props, and merchandising fixtures supplied vendor-direct by Chanel unless otherwise directed in writing. Receipt and set-in-place available as added scope.
  2. 2.8Landlord work. Base-building systems, structural modifications, storefront beyond tenant line, and demising walls excluded. Coordination with landlord contractor carried under General Conditions.
  3. 2.9Permits and fees. Municipal permit and plan-check fees passed through at cost. Expediting services excluded unless directed. Hurricane-zone review fees tracked separately.
  4. 2.10Security and AV systems. Security, EAS, CCTV, and AV vendor-direct install and commissioning excluded from GC scope. Rough-in, blocking, and power provisions included.
03
Approach

Zero retail disruption. Long-lead procurement released on award.

Four operational commitments for the Chanel Miami engagement. Emphasis on discretion, after-hours coordination, and single-point accountability.

3.1

Early procurement.

Millwork and stone packages released immediately upon award. Vendor lead times for Chanel brand-standard casework track to [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] weeks from approved shop drawings. Stone slab selection, templating, and fabrication run in parallel with early site work. Procurement calendar issued within five business days of notice to proceed.

3.2

Night and extended hours.

Hours held to the landlord-mandated operating schedule. Noisy and dusty trades sequenced into after-hours windows as defined by the lease and property management. Daytime activity limited to quiet finish work, millwork dry-fits, and deliveries cleared with building security. Overtime and premium-time carried in General Conditions where noted.

3.3

On-site mockups.

Finish and lighting mockups built on site ahead of full installation. Stone joints, plaster texture, paint sheen, and metal finish samples reviewed in place under specified lighting before release to production. Chanel brand approvals captured in writing and filed against the issued specifications. Mockup assemblies retained for final punch reference.

3.4

Single point of contact.

One point of contact from LBCO through handover. Dedicated Project Manager and Superintendent assigned to this boutique for the full duration — no rotation across other jobs during critical path. Weekly report issued every Friday with photographs, schedule position, cost position, outstanding RFIs, and a two-week look-ahead. One inbox. One number.

01
Overview

Static Chanel retail environment, Miami, priced against an incoming drawing package.

A rough costing exercise for a non-pop-up Chanel build-out in Miami. Scope will be locked once architectural drawings, finish schedule, and Chanel brand standards arrive.

Observation 01

Static Miami build-out, not a pop-up.

The project is a permanent Chanel retail environment in Miami. Full brand-standard execution applies across shell, millwork, stone, metal, and light. Temporary-install methods are not in scope.

Observation 02

Chanel finish tolerances are tighter than commercial retail.

Millwork reveals, stone joinery, metal returns, and lighting alignment are held to Chanel brand-standard specifications. The tolerance band is narrower than the commercial luxury retail norm. Pricing reflects that standard, not a commercial-grade equivalent.

Observation 03

Miami climate and Miami-Dade permitting are schedule inputs.

Coastal humidity affects material selection and adhesive performance. Hurricane season affects storefront protection and delivery windows. Miami-Dade permitting timelines are factored into the phase schedule rather than absorbed after award.

Observation 04

Scope locks when drawings, finish schedule, and brand standards arrive.

This page is a rough order-of-magnitude costing exercise, not a formal bid. Line items, allowances, and phase durations will be reissued once the Chanel document set is received and the Miami site walk is complete.

Scope

Five trades carried. Four carve-outs named.

Included and excluded scope stated in the present tense pending drawing confirmation. Line items reissue once the Chanel document set arrives.

Included — Five Trades Carried
  1. General ConditionsFull-time project manager and site superintendent on the floor for the duration of the build. Site logistics, safety, daily cleanup, and trade coordination carried in base.
  2. DemolitionSelective demolition of existing conditions to prepare shell for Chanel fit-out. Protection of adjacent tenants and common-area finishes included.
  3. Millwork and FixturesChanel brand-standard millwork install coordinated with the approved fabricator. Field-verification, dry-fit, and reveal alignment held to spec.
  4. MEPMechanical, electrical, plumbing rough and finish delivered to Chanel lighting and comfort standards. Controls and circuiting to brand specification.
  5. FinishesStone, metal, wall, and floor finishes installed per Chanel specification package. Mockups precede production installation.
Excluded — Four Carve-Outs Named
  1. FF&E ProcurementFurniture, fixtures, and equipment assumed client-furnished. Receiving, staging, and install-assist available as an added line if requested.
  2. Landlord WorkBase building upgrades, structural reinforcement, and core systems outside demising walls are carried by the landlord and excluded here.
  3. Permits and Impact FeesHard permit costs and Miami-Dade municipal impact fees carried as allowances or by owner. Expediter coordination available as an add.
  4. Security, IT, and POSLow-voltage, intrusion, CCTV, IT, and POS systems handled by Chanel vendors. LBCO provides pathway, rough-in, and coordination only.
03
Approach

Four operating decisions that protect the Chanel finish standard in Miami.

How the team is staffed, how long-leads are tracked, how quality is signed off, and when the noisy work happens.

01

Dedicated luxury retail team.

The job is staffed with heritage-brand flagship veterans, not rotating commercial-retail crews. The principal, project manager, and superintendent are named at award and stay named through punch.

02

Long-lead tracking locked within two weeks of award.

Stone, metal, and specialty lighting are identified, ordered, and tracked in the first fourteen days. Procurement sits with the project manager and is reported weekly against the phase schedule.

03

Mockup-first quality control.

Finish mockups are built and signed off by the Chanel Construction Lead before production installation begins. Tolerance, reveal, color, and joinery are resolved on the mockup, not on the floor.

04

Off-hours work windows planned in.

Noise- and delivery-sensitive work is scheduled around Miami retail corridor landlord and neighbor restrictions. Night deliveries, early pours, and weekend installs are written into the phase plan rather than absorbed later.

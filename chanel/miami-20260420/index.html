Scope of Work

Five work packages included, four excluded from LBCO responsibility.

Scope boundaries are drawn for a client-supplied-millwork model typical of Chanel boutique fit-outs. Detail resolves once drawings and specifications are received.

Included — LBCO Responsibility
  • General Conditions Supervision, project management, site logistics, jobsite protection, temporary utilities, daily clean, and landlord coordination for the duration of the build-out. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] hours and shift assumptions.
  • Demolition Selective demolition of existing conditions per the drawings to be issued. Includes removal, cart-out, and disposal within landlord-approved windows. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] landlord hours and dumpster staging.
  • Millwork Installation Receive, stage, protect, and install Chanel-supplied millwork. Coordinate delivery sequencing, blocking, and shim tolerances to Chanel standard. Fabrication is carried by the Chanel-nominated millworker and excluded here.
  • MEP Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing modifications per the issued design. Includes tie-ins to base-building services, final connections, testing, and balancing. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] service capacities and landlord tap fees.
  • Finishes Flooring, wall finishes, ceiling, and paint per the Chanel finish schedule. Mock-up panels and benchmark reviews precede production work. Stone, specialty plaster, and decorative metals carried to Chanel specification.
Excluded — By Others
  • Permits and Fees Assumed reimbursable at cost or procured by others. Expediter, plan-check, and impact fees are not carried in this ROM. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] municipal fee schedule and expediter allowance.
  • FF&E Furniture, fixtures, and equipment procured and installed by Chanel-nominated vendors. LBCO carries receiving coordination only where specifically requested.
  • Security and AV Security hardware, intrusion, CCTV, and AV systems provided and installed by the Chanel-nominated specialists. Rough-in coordination with those vendors is carried within LBCO general conditions.
  • Landlord Work Base-building items — structural modifications, roof penetrations beyond landlord allowance, demising wall delivery — remain the landlord's responsibility. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] landlord work letter.

Scope in and scope out will be reconciled against Chanel design-team drawings and specifications upon receipt. The above reflects a typical client-supplied-millwork model and should be treated as a framing assumption, not a commitment.

Method

Four operating disciplines carry this project from precon to turnover.

01

Preconstruction alignment.

Budget and schedule are validated against the Chanel design team drawings before trade buyout begins. LBCO issues a reconciliation memo inside ten working days of drawing receipt, flagging scope gaps, long-lead conflicts, and any divergence from this ROM. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] reconciliation deliverable date tied to drawing issuance.

02

Working hours.

Standard-hours versus after-hours work is confirmed against landlord rules and mall or street-tenancy restrictions before pricing is locked. Loading windows, noise cut-offs, and common-area protection drive the general-conditions line and the demolition line directly. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] landlord work letter, loading dock hours, and quiet-hour restrictions.

03

Long-lead tracking.

An active long-lead log is maintained from day one covering Chanel-supplied millwork, stone slabs, specialty plaster, decorative metals, and critical MEP equipment. Dates are tracked against the master schedule, not procurement promises, and any slip is escalated to the Chanel construction lead within the same week. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] item-level lead time matrix.

04

Quality control.

Mock-ups and benchmark reviews are confirmed as prerequisites to production installs. Level-5 drywall panels, paint, specialty plaster, stone set, and millwork scribes are each reviewed in place and signed by the Chanel design team before crews advance. No production work begins on a finish system that has not cleared benchmark.

Phase 01 Intent

Demo and rough-in establishes the shell and the hidden infrastructure. The intent is to clear existing conditions, reset utilities, and land every in-wall inspection before any finish crew steps on site. Durations remain open pending landlord hours and scope confirmation.

Phase 02 Intent

Finishes and millwork install is the longest sequence and carries the most sign-offs. The intent is to clear each benchmark — paint, plaster, stone, millwork scribes — before advancing the next. Chanel design presence is required at every benchmark review.

Phase 03 Intent

Punch and turnover closes the work against the Chanel quality standard rather than a fixed date. The intent is to hand a resolved, cleaned, documented store to Chanel operations with closeout binder and digital record. Duration absorbs any benchmark rework identified late.

01
Overview

Three floors, 49,200 square feet, six half-floor phases.

Preliminary budgetary estimate for the Chanel Beverly Hills office and workshop renovation. Figures issued pre-walkthrough and pre-formal-drawings.

This document prices a 49,200 square foot interior renovation across three floors of the Chanel Beverly Hills property, averaging 16,400 square feet per floor. Scope covers interior demolition, wall reconfiguration, electrical relocation, carpet tile flooring, paint, and acoustical ceiling tile. Work is sequenced in six half-floor phases with one-week overlaps and a two-week holiday buffer, compressing the full run to approximately 33 weeks. The grand total against current assumptions lands at $4,186,770, inclusive of 12% overhead and 5% contingency carried at the trade level.

Three operating constraints govern the number. The Chanel watchmaking workshop remains active through the entire build, which pushes demolition to off-hours per floor at an $8 per square foot labor premium. All other trades run standard daytime hours to hold labor cost at baseline. Wall linear footage is pending plan confirmation and remains the largest single variable in the total; the estimate carries 250 LF per floor at $185 per LF as a directional figure to be reconciled against stamped drawings.

Pricing was drafted from schematic sketches supplied by the Chanel master watchmaker rather than formal architectural drawings. Every line below is directional. A site walkthrough, a Matterport scan across all three floors, and a confirmed wall LF count are the three inputs that convert this preliminary figure into a firm bid. None of the three have been completed yet. All three are owned by LBCO and scheduled in Section 08.

The document is organized as a per-floor rollup, a three-floor rollup, a phased schedule, and a next-steps log. Read in that order. Numbers were drafted on the April planning call from Springfield, New Jersey and reflect the scope as understood on that date.

03
Approach

Off-hours demo, standard-hours build, zero workshop disruption.

Four operating principles govern how the 33-week schedule lands without interrupting active watchmaking operations at Chanel Beverly Hills.

01

Half-floor phasing across six increments.

Six phases of half a floor each, sequenced floor by floor with one-week overlaps between consecutive phases. The overlap compresses total duration to approximately 33 weeks without accelerating any individual trade or compounding overtime premiums.

Each phase runs the same sequence — demolition, then build and sand, then finishes, then punch. The crew cadence stays constant floor to floor, which reduces mobilization cost and keeps the schedule legible to the Chanel operations team running the workshop above and below active work.

02

Off-hours demolition, standard-hours build.

Demolition runs after operating hours on each floor to protect the active Chanel watchmaking workshop from dust, noise, and egress interference. The off-hours premium is reflected in the $8 per square foot demo rate and is carried only where it is strictly required.

All other trades — framing, electrical, flooring, paint, ACT — operate on standard daytime hours. This holds labor costs at baseline across the 33-week run and prevents the full scope from absorbing off-hours premiums unnecessarily.

03

Two-week holiday buffer absorbed into schedule.

The 33-week duration includes an approximate two-week buffer spanning the year-end holiday period. This absorbs the typical slowdown in subcontractor availability, material delivery windows, and Chanel operational capacity without pushing substantial completion into the following quarter.

The buffer is built in, not added on. Schedule risk is distributed across the run rather than concentrated at the end. If the holiday slowdown compresses naturally, the buffer converts to schedule margin against downstream phases.

Assumptions

Six conditions that shaped this number.

Every figure below rests on these pre-walkthrough assumptions. Any change moves the total. Each item closes at walkthrough or with stamped drawings.

  1. Schematic-level plans only.

    Floor plans provided on the April planning call are sketches produced by the Chanel master watchmaker. No formal architectural drawings exist yet, so every figure in this document is directional and subject to refinement once stamped drawings arrive from the Chanel design team or an outside architect of record.

  2. Approximately 49,200 SF across three floors.

    Approximately 16,400 square feet per floor, derived from plan dimensions reviewed on the April call. Floor areas assumed equal for pricing purposes; actual per-floor variance will be reconciled at site walkthrough against a Matterport scan or a tape measurement of each level.

  3. Operational workshop must keep running.

    The Chanel watchmaking workshop remains active throughout construction with no relocation of personnel or equipment. Demolition is priced off-hours per floor at the $8 per SF rate to protect operations. All other trades run standard hours to hold labor cost at baseline without compounding overtime.

  4. Wall LF is the largest single unknown.

    Linear footage of new and reconfigured walls is pending plan review. Estimate carries 250 LF per floor at $185 per LF, or $46,250 per floor and $138,750 across the three floors. Actual count from stamped drawings will move the total more than any other single line item in the document.

  5. Last renovation circa 2007.

    Existing finishes believed to date from approximately 2007, to be confirmed at site walkthrough. Demolition pricing assumes standard office construction with no hazardous material abatement. Abatement scope added only if asbestos, lead paint, or similar conditions surface during the walkthrough or a subsequent environmental screen.

  6. Open-shop labor assumed.

    Non-union labor baseline across all trades. Union labor would increase costs significantly across demo, framing, electrical, and finishes, and requires explicit written client direction before a firm bid is issued. The differential against the current number has not been priced and would be developed on request.

Phase 01 runs eight weeks rather than six to absorb initial mobilization, subcontractor onboarding to the Chanel security and access protocols, and the first off-hours demo cycle against an active floor. Phases 02 through 04 run six weeks each at steady-state cadence. Phases 05 and 06 compress to five weeks each as Floor 3 carries a slightly smaller active-workshop footprint and demo cycles run with an established crew.

One-week overlaps between consecutive phases mean the trailing phase begins punch while the leading phase begins demo on the next half-floor. This requires coordination between the site superintendent and the Chanel operations lead on each floor but does not compound trade costs. The two-week holiday buffer sits between Phases 04 and 05 by default; it moves based on actual start date and is confirmed against the calendar at mobilization.

Gross duration sums to 36 weeks across the six phases. Net duration with overlaps lands at approximately 33 weeks. Substantial completion is measured at Phase 06 punch close. Final turnover follows LBCO standard close-out procedures within two weeks of punch close.

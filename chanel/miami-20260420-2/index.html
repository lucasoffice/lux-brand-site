N° 01 — Project

A permanent Chanel boutique in Miami, scoped ahead of issued documents.

Static retail buildout. Full brand-standard finish package. Pricing issued as a rough order-of-magnitude study pending receipt of architectural drawings, finish schedules, and MEP requirements.

Chanel Miami is a permanent retail buildout. The delivery target is 2026. The footprint is [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] SF across a single trading floor with supporting back-of-house. The location sits within the greater Miami market; the precise municipal jurisdiction will be confirmed once the street address is released. This document prices the work as a static installation carrying full brand-standard finish tolerances from day one.

Three observations govern the study. The first: this is a static buildout and every assembly has been priced for long-term durability rather than short-run pop-up economics. The second: Chanel finish standards demand museum-grade tolerances, custom millwork, specialty lighting integration, and stone and veneer work executed to showroom fit. The third: Miami imposes hurricane-code compliance on exterior-facing assemblies, permanent humidity control on the interior environment, and a municipal permitting cycle that runs longer than comparable North American markets.

The figures that follow are a rough costing exercise. They are not a formal bid. They are not a GMP commitment. They are issued in advance of architectural drawings, finish schedules, and MEP documentation, and will be reconciled against those documents upon receipt. Confidentiality applies to every line. The document is prepared for internal Chanel review and is not intended for external distribution.

Lux Brand Co has prepared the study on the basis of completed luxury retail installations of comparable scope. Figures have been carried at the high end of the reasonable range where documentation is absent. Nothing in this file should be read as a committed price. Everything in this file will be reissued as a formal GMP once complete construction documents are in hand.

Single-shift execution, dedicated Miami team, pre-punch mockup review.

Three operating assumptions governing schedule, staffing, and quality control on the Chanel Miami boutique.

01

Single-Shift Execution

Standard business-hours work confirmed as the baseline assumption. Premium-time work, night shifts, and weekend mobilizations are priced separately if mandated by landlord rules of conduct or centre operating hours. Dedicated Miami Team

Full-time superintendent and project manager assigned through punch and turnover. No rotation off the project. The team holds continuous relationships with the Chanel project lead, the architect of record, and the landlord construction representative across the full duration of the build.

Luxury QC Protocol

Pre-punch mockup reviews and brand-standard finish inspections are embedded in the schedule as formal milestones rather than optional check-ins. Documentation Posture

Daily reports, two-week look-ahead schedules, and weekly RFI logs issued to the Chanel project lead on a fixed cadence. Submittals, samples, and shop drawings tracked against a single register that travels with the project and is archived at turnover for future reference.

Humidity Control

Permanent dehumidification confirmed as a continuous interior requirement. Space set points held through substantial completion and handed off to Chanel facilities team at turnover.

Acclimation period confirmed for specialty veneers and stone prior to install. Millwork deliveries staged to align with HVAC commissioning and interior conditioning.

Municipal Permitting

City of Miami Beach or City of Miami jurisdiction to be confirmed upon receipt of the street address. Plan review cycles assumed at the longer end of recent comparable projects.

Permit fees and municipal charges carried as reimbursable at cost. Expediter retention priced separately and issued on request once jurisdiction is fixed.

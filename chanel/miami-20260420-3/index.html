Overview

A static build-out for Chanel Miami.

Permanent boutique construction carried to House-standard finishes, mockup-approved before fabrication. Rough costing issued ahead of drawings and site visit.

This document responds to a request for a rough costing exercise on a permanent Chanel boutique in Miami, targeted for delivery in 2026. The scope is a static retail build-out. There is no pop-up logic, no temporary overlay, no phased reopening — the store will go dark on a defined date, construction will run as a single continuous sequence, and the space will hand over as a finished Chanel boutique ready for merchandise load-in and House vitrine installation by Chanel's appointed vendors.

The numbers on the following pages are preliminary. They are issued in advance of permit drawings, in advance of a site walk, and in advance of subcontractor buyout. They carry a working assumption of House-standard finishes — stone, metal, specialty millwork, and lighting calibrated to the specification Chanel applies to its boutiques globally. Where a figure cannot yet be resolved against drawings, it is flagged [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] and held back rather than guessed into place.

LUX Brand Company is positioning for the General Contractor role. Scope splits below reflect a standard Chanel turnover condition, with FF&E and branded vitrines furnished and installed by House-designated vendors and the architectural shell, finishes, MEP distribution, and lighting infrastructure carried by LBCO. The intent of this document is alignment — on scope, on mode, on the boundary between builder and House vendor — before drawings are released and a formal bid is requested.

Single-Phase Static Build

Continuous construction sequence without phased reopenings. The store goes dark on the agreed mobilization date and remains closed through to handover, with no interim merchandise moves and no temporary reopen obligations carried in the schedule. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING]

02

Standard Shift Hours

Daytime work carried as the baseline wherever landlord rules and municipal ordinances allow. Premium-time shifts, night work, and weekend hours priced only where the building work letter, corridor access rules, or mall operating covenants explicitly require them. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING]

Early Long-Lead Procurement

Stone, specialty millwork, and custom metalwork released for fabrication at GMP execution rather than at drawing issue. South Florida stone lead times and domestic metal shop capacity both favor early release to protect the critical path and the handover date. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING]

On-site mockups for every governing finish — stone joinery, metal patina, wall plaster, lighting trim — reviewed and signed off with the Chanel design team before full fabrication is released. No finish runs without a mockup on the wall. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING]

04
Miami Conditions

Hurricane code, South Florida lead times, landlord work letter.

Three variables specific to the Miami market that shape cost, schedule, and sequencing on this static build.

Code

Miami-Dade hurricane code governs storefront glazing, exterior anchorage, and any roof-mounted mechanical equipment. Impact-rated glass assemblies, tested fastening details, and High Velocity Hurricane Zone documentation add both hard cost and submittal time to any storefront or exterior metal package.

Interior partitions and finish scope are less affected, but the building envelope points — entry doors, display windows, service doors, rooftop curbs — must be coordinated between the architect, the storefront vendor, and the mechanical engineer early enough that product data and NOA numbers are in hand before permit submission. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING]

Labor

South Florida subcontractor capacity is tight across stone, specialty metal, and finish plaster. Miami trades are absorbing sustained hospitality and luxury retail demand across Brickell, the Design District, Bal Harbour, and Aventura, and lead times for qualified shops run measurably longer than Northeast or West Coast benchmarks.

Pricing assumes a tier of subcontractors already familiar with the Chanel finish standard or demonstrably capable of achieving it against mockup. Unqualified low bids are not carried. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] week lead time is assumed on imported stone and [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] weeks on custom metalwork.

Permitting

Miami municipal permit timelines for tenant build-outs of this class and finish level run [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING] weeks from drop to pickup, with a separate plan review track required for HVHZ components. Expediter engagement is standard on boutique work and is carried in the General Conditions allowance.

The schedule assumes permit is secured before mobilization and before any long-lead fabrication is released for installation. Preconstruction is sized to absorb expected review comment cycles without disturbing the construction start date. [UPDATE BEFORE SENDING]

Project Understanding

We heard you.
Here is what we understand.

This project carries a layered strategy that extends well beyond a standard buildout. The phased permitting approach, the back-of-house redesign mid-stream, and the coordination between multiple vendors touching millwork, stone, and storefront all demand a GC that can think ahead and adapt without losing control of the details.

i — Phased Permitting

Phased Permitting

Award on current permit drawings. Amendment drawings expected early May with revised back-of-house layout and reduced gross square footage. We are prepared to begin work on the current scope while maintaining flexibility to reprice transparently as the design evolves.

ii — Multi-Party Coordination

Multi-Party Coordination

One vendor produces the millwork. Another installs it. A third delivers it. Add the storefront vendor, the owner-supplied stone installation, and the back-of-house reconfiguration. We thrive in this complexity.

iii — Design Integrity

Design Integrity

We visited the Miami Design District location to study the concept firsthand. The light slots, floor pattern alignment, wall finishes, and millwork all follow strict visual cues. Aventura has a flat ceiling and no perimeter cove, but the same standard of alignment applies.

iv — HQ Alignment

HQ Alignment

Every communication from the field is structured to be forwarded without editing. Clean numbers, clear notes, no ambiguity. When an issue surfaces, you hear it from us first — with a recommended resolution already attached.

01   General Requirements

Ben raised
Have the subcontractor absorb the flagged line. If not, roll it into the VCT scope.
LBCO
Absorbed by the subcontractor. $1,020 removed from the bid form entirely.

04   Masonry — Landlord Corridor Tile

Ben raised
Reduce the masonry number to reflect the shorter storefront. Four rows of landlord corridor tile are approved, but only in front of the immediate storefront.
LBCO
13% reduction applied to both material supply from the mall and install labor to match the shortened run.

06   Wood & Plastic — Framing + Plywood

Ben raised
Storefront scope was reduced; plywood sheathing cost should come down accordingly.
LBCO
Plywood deduct for the 20' storefront reduction was captured by the subcontractor. The redesign added walls and framing elsewhere, producing a $12,300 increase in framing and drywall overall. Net: the division moves up, not down.

07   Thermal & Moisture — RTU Eliminated

Ben raised
One RTU was eliminated; this number should reduce. Will need a second look later.
LBCO
Revised to reflect fewer roof penetrations in the current design. Will revise again when the final roof plan locks.

11   Equipment — Day-2 Dishwasher

Ben raised
Dishwasher removed from drawings to avoid the DERM permit per landlord. Include Day-2 install after inspections, plus a temporary blank millwork panel and an allowance for retrofit. Touches Equipment, Plumbing, Electrical, and Millwork.
LBCO
Dishwasher carried in the equipment scope. Plumbing now includes a return trip and materials for post-C of O install. Temporary blank millwork panel included.

17   HVAC — Credit

Ben raised
The credit looks low. The removed unit combo sat in a space the client wasn't using and had no ductwork attached — the credit should be higher.
LBCO
These are small, off-the-shelf units — not custom-fabricated air handlers. The credit reflects their unit value, not a markup on scope. Holding at the current number.

18   Electrical — 131 + 6 Drivers

Ben raised
Confirm 131 drivers + 6 spares are included. LBCO and Jasmine to coordinate.
LBCO
Revised up from 114 + 5 spares. Additional driver supply added to the electrical line.

Mechanical — Diffusers

Ben raised
Linear diffusers appeared reduced by ~30 LF; confirm that reduction is captured.
LBCO
Respectfully — diffusers did not come down. Plenum boxes moved from 20 to 22; ACT diffusers from 5 to 8. Ductwork was extended to cover the redesigned footprint, so the mechanical total reflects a modest increase, not a reduction.

Landlord Tactile Signage — Rear Door

Ben raised
Landlord requires a tenant ID tactile sign at the rear door. Confirm it's included.
LBCO
Included in specialties from the original bid. No change required.

Floor Leveling — VCT-to-Tile Transition

Ben raised
Flush transition required between VCT and front-of-house tile. No level change permitted.
LBCO
Means and methods carried by LBCO. Subs have pricing locked for the flush detail; the number is covered as-is.

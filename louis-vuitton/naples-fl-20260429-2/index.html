A schematic-stage anchor against the $1M–$5M internal spread.

Leadership has heard figures from one to five million for the Naples three-floor renovation. This document fixes a defensible number at $4,186,770 before plans are drawn.

02
Scope / Portée

Demolition, partitions, electrical relocation, and finishes across ~49,200 SF.

Nine work items confirmed in. Five items carved out to keep the GC base clean for leadership review.

Included / Inclus
Demolition
Interior office finishes demo, approximately one week per floor. Daytime hours confirmed by client.
Wall Reconfiguration
New partition walls per schematic markup. Budgeted at ~250 LF per floor pending plan confirmation.
Electrical Relocation
Lump sum per floor for outlet, lighting, and circuit relocation tied to the new wall layout.
Carpet Tile
20" × 20" carpet tile in office areas, plus adhesive at 15% of material cost.
Specialty VCT
White rubberized VCT in watchmaker work areas on Floor 2, so dropped components bounce and remain visible.
Epoxy Floor
Approximately 70% of Floor 1 ground, scraped, and finished in epoxy-style paint for warehouse use.
Paint
Coat of paint throughout renovated areas; included in the finishes labor rate.
ACT Patching
Salvage and patch existing acoustic ceiling tile around new wall locations.
Waste & Dumpsters
Approximately five dumpsters per phase across demo and finishes.
Excluded / Exclu
Furniture Manipulation
Carved to a separate furniture vendor scope.
Stainless Workbenches & Jeweler Stations
Specialty equipment procured under client equipment budget.
Architectural & Design Drawings
Client scope. Not included in GC pricing.
Permit-Driven Expansions
Base estimate assumes finishes-only classification. Sprinkler or electrical thresholds would add est. $15–20K.
Wallpaper & Specialty Wall Finishes
Confirmed out by client. Paint only.
Wallpaper and specialty wall finishes confirmed out — paint only.

Approach / Méthode

Six phases, half-floor at a time, daytime demo confirmed.

Phasing protects operations across watchmaking, jewelers, polishing, and ops. Off-hours site investigation preserves staff confidentiality.

01

Half-Floor Phasing

Six phases, half a floor each. One week demo, two weeks build and sand, two weeks finishes, one week punch. Roughly one week of overlap between phases.

02

Daytime Demo Confirmed

Standard hours confirmed by client. No overnight premium carried in this figure. Demo runs during the working day across all three floors.

03

Off-Hours Site Investigation

Walkthrough and Matterport scan run after hours. Broader Naples staff are not informed of the planning effort.

04

Open-Shop Subcontractor Base

Lux Brand Co's existing relationships in the Naples and Southwest Florida corridor. Non-union, competitive labor rates.

05

Light-Touch Construction Set

RCP, demo, and one construction page per floor. Classified as finishes work to keep clear of full permit submission where possible.

06

Living Budget Microsite

This page is the working document. Chris and Brian post questions and changes between meetings rather than via telephone updates.

04
Constraints / Contraintes

Schematic-only plans, freight elevator stops at Floor 1, confidentiality required.

Three operational realities shape every number on this page. Each is named so the budget can be read against them.

Plans / Plans

Floor plans exist only as schematic sketches by the master watchmaker. Three second-floor options remain in play. Alignment with Chris and Brian is pending before any number can tighten.

Logistics / Logistique

The freight elevator reaches only Floor 1. Second-floor storage requires manual repacking, which informs both phasing and crew sizing. Material staging is built around this constraint.

Confidentiality / Confidentialité

Broader Naples staff are not to be informed. Walkthroughs and Matterport scans occur off-hours. Any on-site presence is coordinated through Jacquelyn.

Rough Costing Exercise — Not a Formal Bid

This exercise exists because leadership at louis vuitton is holding two competing internal numbers and no drawings. Operations has carried the work at $1M to $1.5M. The President has carried the same building at roughly $5M. Neither figure was built from a measured plan, and the gap is wide enough to stall the decision to commission architectural fees.

The document below assembles a defensible middle-ground figure for louis vuitton from per-floor unit rates, a 250 LF per floor partition placeholder, and lump-sum allowances for electrical relocation. Quantities are conversation-based, drawn from circa-2007 base sheets and hand-drawn blue-line markups from the master watchmaker. The intent is a number leadership can use to authorize drawings, not a number to sign a contract against.

The base building is three floors at approximately 16,400 SF each. Floor 1 becomes primary storage, relocated from Floor 2, with roughly 70% scraped and epoxy-painted concrete and 30% finished office area. Floor 2 is rebuilt around the watchmaker workflow with white rubberized VCT in the bench zones, new offices, and reconfigured electrical to follow the new layout.

What this document is not: a formal bid, a contract, or a fixed price. The grand total of $4,186,770 reflects a per-floor build-up rolled to three floors, plus 12% overhead and 5% contingency, against scope as currently understood. Wall linear footage, the Floor 2 schematic selection, and the permit path are still open and each carries quantified swing into the next pricing pass.

The scope assumes interior demolition, partition reconfiguration, electrical relocation, flooring across all three finish profiles, paint, and ACT patching. Furniture, stainless steel watchmaker benches, polishing equipment, and architectural drawings are routed elsewhere so the GC scope reads cleanly. Springfield, NJ is open shop; trade relationships are held by Sean.

The number will move. It will move down if the second-floor schematic chosen reduces partition count or if the finishes-only permit path holds. It will move up if sprinkler or electrical work crosses the threshold that triggers a permit filing, or if wall linear footage exceeds the carried 250 LF per floor. Both directions are quantified in the constraints and guardrails sections that follow.

Approach

Phased half-floor execution, budget-first, drawings-second.

Six phases at half-floor each with ~1 week overlap to maintain operational continuity. Walkthrough handled off-hours or by Matterport scan so internal staff are not alerted to the project.

A1

Phased Half-Floor Execution

Six phases at half-floor each with approximately one week of overlap to maintain operational continuity. Demo runs during the day; no overnight work is required per updated client direction.

Each half moves through demo, build/sand, finishes, and punch in sequence. The active half of each floor stays operational while the renovation half is sealed, dust-contained, and HVAC-isolated.

A2

Budget-First, Drawings-Second

Deliver a defensible ballpark to leadership now so a go or no-go decision can be made before architectural fees are committed. The budget refines as schematics firm up and wall linear footage is confirmed against the selected second-floor option.

The pricing rollup is built from per-floor unit rates and is structured to absorb plan refinements without rebuilding the model. Each refinement updates a single line.

A3

Confidential Walkthrough

Off-hours site walk or Matterport scan so internal staff are not alerted to the louis vuitton project. Imagery is held to named stakeholders, and no photography is permitted of watchmaker or polishing workstations during the visit.

If the off-hours window cannot be cleared, the Matterport scan substitutes for the physical walk and produces a measurable record for the next pricing pass.

A4

Shared Planning Hub

A Lux-hosted micro-site for stakeholders Jacquelyn, Chris, and Brian to drop input asynchronously. Lux compiles and republishes revisions to reduce telephone-game churn between brand, design, and GC.

The hub holds the pricing model, the timeline, the constraints register, and the running list of open items so that every stakeholder reads from the same document at any moment.

A5

Furniture Decoupled

GC scope stays clean by routing furniture and stainless workstations through a separate vendor. The decoupling avoids change-order risk on equipment whose final layout, power requirements, and physical dimensions are not yet defined.

Coordination between the GC and the furniture vendor is held by Claudia, who carried the same role on the Miami project and is praised by the client for that work.

A6

Permit Minimization

File as finishes work where defensible. Pre-vet sprinkler and electrical thresholds with trades before committing the permit path so the scope of architectural drawings is sized correctly the first time.

A drawing add of $15K–$20K is carried as a contingency if a permit filing is triggered by sprinkler relocation or by electrical work that crosses the local threshold.

Springfield, NJ is open shop; trade relationships are held by Sean.

Constraints

Three open variables shape the final number.

Wall linear footage, the Floor 2 schematic selection, and the permit path each carry meaningful budget swing. This section names them so the rollup that follows is read with the right tolerances.

Wall LF

250 LF per floor carried as placeholder; each additional 100 LF adds approximately $18,500 at $185 per LF.

Swing — ±$18,500 per 100 LF

Resolved against the chosen second-floor schematic and a verified take-off from updated plans. Until that point, the carried figure represents an industry-typical density for office and workshop reconfiguration.

Floor 2 Schematic

Three second-floor options are still in play; selection drives demo, partition, and electrical relocation totals across the watchmaker zones.

Swing — Demo, Partition, Electrical

The three options differ in bench count, office footprint, and the location of the polishing area. Each option carries a different partition density and a different electrical relocation profile against the existing service.

Permit Path

Filed as finishes-only where defensible; sprinkler or electrical above threshold doubles drawing scope and adds approximately $15K–$20K.

Swing — +$15K to $20K

Pre-vetted with the electrical and sprinkler trades before drawings are commissioned so the architectural scope is sized correctly. The threshold turns on the extent of sprinkler relocation and the size of the electrical service work.

Guardrails

Wall LF and permit path are the two budget swings.

Both are quantified. Both can be resolved before the next pricing pass.

Wall Linear Footage

Each additional 100 LF adds ~$18,500.

The budget carries 250 LF per floor as a placeholder. Each additional 100 LF of partition adds approximately $18,500 at the carried $185 per LF rate. The figure is to be confirmed against the chosen second-floor schematic before commitment, since the three options in play differ meaningfully in partition density.

Permit Path

Sprinkler or electrical above threshold doubles drawing scope.

If sprinkler relocation or electrical exceeds threshold, the project must be filed for permit and architectural drawings roughly double in scope. A $15K–$20K drawing add is carried as a contingency if that path is triggered.

The path is pre-vetted with the sprinkler and electrical trades before drawings are commissioned so the architectural scope is sized correctly the first time and the permit posture holds through construction.

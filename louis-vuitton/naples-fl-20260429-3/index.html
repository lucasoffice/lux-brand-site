01
Overview

A two-floor renovation for the Springfield watchmaker facility, costed before drawings.

Preliminary budgetary exercise covering Floors 1 and 2 of the Springfield, New Jersey facility — approximately 32,800 square feet total. Floor 3 has been removed from scope per Chris, RVP Operations.

Jacquelyn Albano of LVMH and TAG Heuer is the client lead on this exercise; Luke Shank of Lux Brand Co serves as principal. The intent is to give Jacquelyn a number she can hold up internally before drawings are commissioned.

Leadership has carried an estimate of approximately five million dollars. Operations has carried one and a half to two million for the same scope. This document exists to land a defensible figure between those two numbers, costed against assumptions rather than drawings.

Floor 1 is being repositioned as primarily storage, relocated from Floor 2. Floor 2 will hold the watchmaker, jeweler, polishing, and packaging stations alongside new offices. The renovation will be executed inside a live operation.

02
Scope

Finishes, walls, and electrical relocation across both floors.

Interior demolition, partition reconfiguration, ACT patching, paint, and three flooring systems sized to use. Furniture, stainless workstations, and architectural drawings sit outside this estimate.

Floor 1 becomes primarily storage; Floor 2 holds the watchmaker, jeweler, polishing, and packaging stations with new offices.

Included — 9 items
Interior demolition
Selective demolition of partitions, finishes, and ceiling tiles across both floors. Floor 1 demolition runs during the day; Floor 2 phases tighter around the live watchmaker stations.
Wall construction
New partition walls per the master watchmaker's blue-line markup. Budgeted at approximately 250 linear feet on Floor 2 and 125 linear feet on Floor 1.
Flooring — Floor 2 watchmaker zones
Specialty white rubberized VCT. Light color allows visibility of dropped components; the rubber surface helps small parts bounce rather than break. Specification to be provided by client.
Flooring — Floor 1 offices
Carpet tile, twenty-by-twenty squares, approximately nine dollars per square foot installed plus fifteen percent adhesive, across the office pockets totaling roughly thirty percent of the floor.
Flooring — Floor 1 warehouse
Scrape existing adhesive and apply epoxy paint over concrete across the remaining seventy percent. No grinding required if the existing glue is uniform.
ACT ceiling patching
Patch and replace acoustic ceiling tile and grid as walls are reconfigured. New tile only where the existing material cannot be salvaged.
Paint
One coat throughout renovated areas. No wallpaper. Rolled into the per-square-foot labor rate.
Electrical relocation
Relocation and manipulation of existing circuits to suit the new wall layout. Lump sum per floor; no major service upgrades assumed.
Dumpsters and general conditions
Approximately five dumpsters per phase to support demolition swap-outs, plus standard supervision and site protection.
Excluded — 5 items
Furniture and furniture manipulation
Handled by a separate furniture vendor under their own scope and budget. Keeps GC pricing clean and allows independent vendor selection.
Stainless steel workbenches and jeweler stations
Specialty equipment falls under the operations equipment budget, not GC scope.
Architectural and design drawings
Client-side scope. Lux Brand Co will deliver a working build spec to support the architect, but the drawings themselves are not in this estimate.
Permit and sprinkler modifications
Base estimate assumes a finishes-only classification with no permit pulled. Carried separately if triggered.

03
Approach

Six phases of half-floor work, daytime where the facility allows it.

Phasing protects the master watchmaker team's workflow. The freight elevator only reaches Floor 1, so Floor 2 demolition runs off-hours and material moves are sequenced around live stations.

01

Phased, occupied-building execution.

Six phases of half-floor work hold the facility live throughout. Floor 1 phasing is relaxed because the storage move-in absorbs daytime demolition; Floor 2 phasing is tighter around the watchmaker stations.

02

Daytime labor where possible.

Per client confirmation, no overnight work is required. This reduces premium-time costs against the off-hours assumption carried in the v5 PDF and keeps the labor rate stable across Floor 1.

03

Off-hours walkthrough and Matterport.

Initial site capture is conducted off-hours to keep the project confidential from the internal team. The Matterport scan supports remote stakeholder review without repeated on-site visits.

04

Build spec first, drawings second.

Lux Brand Co issues a working build spec from the costing tool so the architect sketches against a known scope rather than chase clarifications. The design loop compresses; the budget holds shape earlier.

05

Single-source GC, separate furniture vendor.

Lux and Sean carry the construction box. The furniture vendor proposes layout and product separately. Cleaner accountability, fewer middlemen, and the GC number stays defensible without specialty markups.

06

Living document for stakeholder input.

This microsite lets Chris, Brian, and other stakeholders post questions and preferences asynchronously. Lux compiles input into the next revision rather than running parallel telephone games across calendars.

Build-spec sequence

Lux Brand Co issues the working build spec from the costing tool first, so the architect sketches against a known scope. The spec carries assumed wall linear footage, finish allocations, and electrical lump sums; the architect then refines, and the schematic decision locks the variable items in one revision rather than three.

Guardrails

Two posture statements that govern this number.

The estimate is costed against assumptions, not drawings, and it presumes a finishes-only permit posture.

01 — Costing Posture

Costed against assumptions, not drawings.

Square footage (~16,400 SF per floor), wall linear footage (250 on Floor 2, 125 on Floor 1), and the electrical lump sum are working assumptions. Wall LF is the single largest swing variable — the final number locks once a schematic is selected.

02 — Permit Posture

Permit posture is finishes-only.

Base price assumes no permit pulled. If sprinkler heads must move or the electrical scope crosses the code threshold, add approximately $15–20K for drawings plus permit fees and the corresponding schedule impact.

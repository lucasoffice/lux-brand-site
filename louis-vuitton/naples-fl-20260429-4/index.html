Overview

A schematic costing for the Naples maison office and watchmaker floor.

Issued to Jacquelyn Albano to settle internal estimates that have ranged from $1.5M to $5M against a single defensible figure. Basis is hand-marked sketches and verbal scope; no formal architectural drawings exist.

This document was prepared at Jacquelyn's request as a rough costing exercise, not a formal bid. The program covers approximately 49,200 SF across three floors of louis vuitton office and watchmaker workshop space — interior demolition, wall reconfiguration, electrical relocation, ACT patching, paint, carpet tile in office zones, and white rubberized VCT in watchmaker zones.

Chris, RVP Operations, has signaled a scope reduction to Floors 1 and 2 as the active target, with Floor 3 retained in this rollup pending confirmation. The figure below carries all three floors so the reduction can be priced as a credit once the decision is final.

Ten items carried in, five items carried out.

Base labor rate $56/SF; demo $8/SF; walls $185/LF at ~250 LF per floor; electrical $130K lump per floor.

Approach

Six phases, half a floor at a time, demo off-hours.

Demo runs off-hours to protect operations on the watchmaker floor; framing, finishes, and electrical run on standard hours to control labor cost. Each phase carries a one-week overlap with the next.

01
Six-phase, half-floor sequencing.
Half a floor at a time with a one-week overlap. Demo on the next phase begins as finishes complete on the prior.
02
Off-hours demo, standard-hours build.
Demolition runs after-hours to protect watchmaker operations. Framing, finishes, and electrical run on standard hours to hold the labor rate.

Single-week demo per phase.

04
Project Constraints

Eight observations governing the number.

Conditions read at the schematic stage that move cost in either direction once drawings exist. Documented for Jacquelyn before leadership review.

01

Conflicting internal estimates.

Leadership figures have ranged from $1.5M to $5M for the same scope. This exercise stands as the defensible middle figure.

02

Schematic information only.

No architectural drawings exist; the master watchmaker provided hand-marked sketches with three competing second-floor layouts.

03

Freight elevator reaches Floor 1 only.

Crates and material between floors require manual movement, affecting demo logistics and finish delivery.

04

2007-era finishes.

Existing ACT and finish package date to ~2007 per drawing title blocks; condition to be verified during walkthrough.

05

Operational flow is the underlying issue.

Current layout creates dead space and breaks flow between jewelers, watchmakers, polishing, and operations.

06

Scope reduced to two floors.

Per Chris, the renovation now targets Floors 1 and 2; Floor 3 retained in this rollup pending final confirmation.

07

Specialty flooring required.

White rubberized VCT in watchmaker zones so dropped components are visible and bounce rather than break.

08

Open-shop labor in Springfield.

Springfield, NJ confirmed non-union; Lux open-shop subcontractor relationships apply directly.

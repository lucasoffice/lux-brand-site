01
Overview

Two estimates, $1M and $5M, reconciled against real footage.

Jacquelyn Albano commissioned this exercise to replace two unsourced internal numbers with a defensible figure built from linear footage, square footage, and unit rates. The 2007 drawings on file remain the only documented baseline.

01.i

Two estimates with no shared basis.

Leadership has heard $1M from one source and $5M from another for the same renovation. Reconciliation runs against documented footage and unit rates.

01.ii

Workflow drives the program.

Watchmaker, jeweler, polishing, and packaging teams move materials down stairs and re-crate before the freight elevator, which serves only Floor 1. Adjacency, not finish, is the brief.

01.iii

Confidentiality is paramount.

Site visits run off-hours. Staff cannot know a renovation is being scoped, which shapes walkthroughs, demo sequencing, and the single Matterport capture proposed.

01.iv

Open-shop labor base, Miami precedent.

Springfield is non-union. Rates hold predictable against the Lux Brand Co North/Central NJ bench — the same bench that delivered Louis Vuitton Miami.

03
Approach

Six phases, off-hours demo, one Matterport capture.

The sequence rolls half-floor by half-floor with one-week overlaps so watchmaker, jeweler, polishing, and packaging teams relocate in manageable chunks. A single off-hours scan replaces repeat walkthroughs.

03.1

Half-floor sequencing.

Six phases roll up-over-up-over-up-over with one-week overlaps. Trades stay productive while operations relocate in chunks the freight elevator can absorb.

03.2

Off-hours demo where it counts.

Demo on the active watchmaker floor runs off-hours to protect production. Daytime demo holds on Floor 1 once it converts to storage.

03.3

Matterport in lieu of revisits.

One off-hours capture lets every internal stakeholder review the space asynchronously. Staff are not pulled into multiple walkthroughs.

03.4

Build-spec hand-off.

This micro-site publishes a structured build spec for the client-side architect. The design loop compresses; revision cycles drop.

03.5

Floor 1 reframed.

Per 04/15, ~70% of Floor 1 downgrades to scrape-and-epoxy concrete with ~30% office buildout. Per-SF blended cost on that floor drops.

03.6

Furniture decoupled.

Workstations and specialty fixtures move to a separate vendor track. The construction number stays clean and change-order risk stays contained.
